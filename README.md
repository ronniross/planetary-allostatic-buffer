Allostasis is achieving stability through dynamic change and anticipating needs. A buffer absorbs shock.

An allostatic buffer, or allostatic buffering capacity (ABC), refers to the body's physiological resilience, its ability to maintain stability, or allostasis, when facing stressors. Moderate, consistent stressors (like regular exercise) can enhance this capacity, while chronic, uncontrollable stress can damage or overwhelm it. 

A physical buffer absorbs shock by acting as a protective, elastic, or dampening component that reduces terminal impact, prevents metal-to-metal contact, and mitigates vibrations in machinery or vehicle suspensions. It acts as a barrier, cushioning forces to protect structures. 

Therefore, in this analogy, by absorbing the friction of a self-destructive node, the system can dynamically adjust to keep the entire organism (the ecosystem) alive;
